The Search & Replace Editor

SRE is not only a replacement for E.EXE, it also was the first application written in Intelligent Design, coded in 100 percent C-, thread-, push- und pop-free assembler. After replacing two (time consuming) threads, the concerned functions gained some speed. Two functions were added (text modules and scribs), but nevertheless, the new version is smaller than the old one (60 versus 87 KB). SRE never was the slowest editor on the market, but now it is damned fast.
SRE offers the following features:
  • Editing of up to 32 text files simultaneously
  • 32 permanently accessible search/replace-pairs
  • Replace patterns may include line breaks
  • Handling of text files with a size of up to 4 Gigabyte
  • Splitting of huge files into handy parts
  • Conversion of binary files into editable hex dumps and vice versa
  • Global search and replace
    • In up to 1,024 directories with up to 16,384 files
    • Subdirectories are included automatically
    • Up to 32 search/replace-pairs simultaneously
    • Search and replace patterns may include line breaks
    • Search patterns may include wildcards
  • Up to 256 "Scribs" with up to 8,191 characters, each
  • Up to 256 text modules with up to 4,096 characters, each
  • Functions for HTML metatag management (experimental)
  • UnGCC restores hexadecimals converted by GCC (what the heck is -16777216?)
